Think or Say? — a thought into a choice

A scenario appears with a thought the learner is having. The clinician reads it aloud, then the learner decides: should that thought stay inside (THINK IT) or is it kind and okay to say out loud (SAY IT)?

  • Category — focus on one situation type (looks, smells, kind things…) or use all.
  • Order — shuffle the cards or step through them in order.
  • ⚙ Settings — errorless, re-present errors, prompts, and whether to reveal the reason.
  • Prompt — highlights the correct bubble for an assisted response.

Yellow = THINK IT  •  Green = SAY IT. Tap 📖 Learn for a teaching intro, or ▶ Play to jump straight in.

How do I know — Think it or Say it?

Some thoughts we keep inside our head. Some thoughts are kind or helpful, so we can say them out loud.

Before you say a thought, ask yourself:

  • THINK IT Could it hurt someone’s feelings? Keep it inside.
  • THINK IT Is it about something private or embarrassing? Keep it inside.
  • SAY IT Is it kind or a nice compliment? You can say it!
  • SAY IT Is it helpful, or does it keep someone safe? Say it!

Not sure? It’s okay to keep it inside — or ask a grown-up.
